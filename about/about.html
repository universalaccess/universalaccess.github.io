Teaching

  • Spring 2021: MIT Graduate subject, "Electric Power in the Developing World."

    • The course presents an in-depth interdisciplinary perspective of the problems, challenges and opportunities of the electric power sector in developing countries, with regulation providing the link among the engineering, economic, legal and environmental viewpoints. The students will understand the political economy choices made concerning the structure and ownership of the power sectors in representative countries, as well as the underlying reasons for the successes and failures. The course examines the complex technical, financial, regulatory, and social challenges of providing universal electricity access in developing countries. The students will learn the adequate supply and demand technologies to meet the energy needs, the estimation of the energy demand, on-grid and off-grid modes of electrification, existing and innovative business models and regulatory instruments, tariff design, consumer preferences and willingness to pay, and electrification planning techniques. Students will make use of optimization models to plan on-grid and microgrid electricity supply. The course will prepare students to contribute to research, technology deployment, and policymaking for the power sector worldwide – with an emphasis of the developing world –as well as for future careers in industry, government, consulting, or multilateral development organizations.

      Instructors: Ignacio Pérez-Arriaga and Robert Stoner.

  • Spring 2018 - Present: Florence School of Regulation online course "Regulation for universal access to energy." Course website

    • The MIT-Comillas Universal Energy Access Lab maintains an on-going collaboration with the Florence School of Regulation (FSR) at the European University Institute in Florence, Italy, where Professor Ignacio Pérez-Arriaga is one of the co-founders and since 2004 has been the Director of Training and is in charge of the Energy Department of the School.
    • The FSR has been offering twice a year since March 2018 a MOOC course on “Regulation for Sustainable Development Goal 7”1 directed by Professor Ignacio Pérez-Arriaga and with participation of a large number of international professionals including several members of the MIT/Comillas Universal Energy Access Lab.

  • Fall 2018: MIT Graduate subject, "Engineering, economics and regulation of universal energy access in developing countries."

  • Spring 2017: MIT graduate subject, "Planning, Policy, and Technology for Energy Access in Developing Countries" for Spring 2017. Course flyer and website
